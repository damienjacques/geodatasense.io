Our team

Our two experts are both engineers and Ph. D. thesis holders. They have extensive experience in the management, analysis and visualization of geographical data.

Alexandre Maugnard

Expert in Spatial Data Analytics

Alexandre Maugnard has worked more than 12 years in different research insitutions. His research works focused on the management of water and soil resources using geomatics and remote sensing tools. He developped decision support tools for political and environmental managers for assessing flood risk, water pollution, soil degradation, ... He was particularly interested in the issue of the spatial representation of information, changes of scale and their impact on the results of models and decison support tools. He has developed strong skills in many GIS softwares and remote sensing tools but also in programming. He is now working since several years as freelance consultant. He carried out projects in Europe and Africa involving in hydrological modeling, spatial analysis, development of decision support systems, and training for private company, public administration or international organization such as the FAO. Alexandre is an agricultural engineer from the Faculté des Sciences Agronomiques de Gembloux and holds a Ph.D. in Bioengineering and Agronomy from the Université catholique de Louvain.

Damien Jacques

Expert in Data Innovation

Damien Jacques is a freelance consultant in data innovation for development. He has led projects across the globe requiring (i) designing and implementing algorithms to extract key insights from large unstructured data, (ii) develop strategies to leverage the entire data value chain of companies and development agencies; and (iii) successfully scale up data solutions in complex and multi-stakeholder ecosystem. He has established a strong academic track record through several projects in Data Science and AI for social good (including projects in food security, poverty and agricultural economics). His projects involved the use of mobile phone data and satellite images and GIS, machine learning and geostatistics analyses in Central America and Africa. Damien worked for Dalberg Data Insights, the Inter-American Bank of Development, the University of California, Los Angeles, the Université Libre de Bruxelles, and the Université Catholique de Louvain. He was awarded the Agriculture prize in the Data For Development (D4D) challenge organized by Orange, MIT, and the UN. Damien holds a Ph.D. in Bioengineering and Agronomy from the Université catholique de Louvain.
